KEY FACTS ABOUT UTTAR PRADESH

Understanding the culture, history, and people behind the movement.

Language

Hindi

Hindi is the main language spoken across Uttar Pradesh. It is also India's most widely spoken language, with over 500 million speakers nationwide.

Urdu

Urdu is widely spoken, especially in cities like Lucknow. It shares roots with Hindi but uses Persian script and carries a rich literary tradition.

Why It Matters

Language is how culture is passed down. When women are denied education, they lose access to literacy in their own language — cutting them off from history, law, and opportunity.

Traditions & Celebrations

Diwali

The festival of lights is celebrated widely across UP. Families light oil lamps, share sweets, and pray together. It marks the victory of light over darkness.

Holi

Holi originated in UP and is one of India's most joyful festivals. People throw coloured powder and water to celebrate the arrival of spring and the triumph of good over evil.

Eid

Uttar Pradesh has a large Muslim population. Eid is celebrated with prayer, feasts, and community gatherings. It is one of the most important events in the UP calendar.

Why It Matters

Traditions bring communities together — but they can also reinforce roles that limit women. Understanding traditions helps us celebrate what is beautiful while questioning what is harmful.

Food & Cuisine

Biryani

Lucknow's Awadhi biryani is one of the most famous dishes in India. It uses slow-cooking techniques passed down through generations and is known for its delicate spices.

Chaat

Agra and Varanasi are famous for their street food, especially chaat — a mix of crispy dough, yogurt, tamarind, and spices. It is eaten by people from all backgrounds.

Petha

Petha is a famous sweet from Agra made from white pumpkin. It has been made there for centuries and is a symbol of the city's rich food heritage.

Why It Matters

Food is deeply tied to culture and family life. In many UP households, women are responsible for all cooking but have no say in family decisions. Their labour deserves recognition.

Clothing & Dress

Saree

The saree is the traditional dress for women across UP. It is worn in different styles depending on the occasion, region, and religion. It represents grace and cultural identity.

Chikankari

Lucknow is famous for Chikankari — a delicate hand embroidery style on light fabric. Most Chikankari workers are women, though they are often underpaid for their skilled craft.

Kurta Pyjama

Men across UP traditionally wear the kurta pyjama, especially during festivals and ceremonies. It is seen as a symbol of cultural pride and modesty.

Why It Matters

Women in UP are often judged based on how they dress. Clothing is used to enforce silence and conformity. True cultural pride means celebrating dress without using it to control women.

Heritage & History

The Taj Mahal

Built in Agra in the 1600s, the Taj Mahal is one of the Seven Wonders of the World. It was built by Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal.

Varanasi

Varanasi is one of the oldest cities in the world, estimated to be over 3,000 years old. It sits on the banks of the Ganges River and is one of Hinduism's holiest sites.

The Mughal Empire

Much of modern UP was the heartland of the Mughal Empire. Cities like Agra and Lucknow were centres of art, architecture, poetry, and culture during this era.

Why It Matters

UP has a history rich with beauty and achievement. But history also shows that women were rarely given credit. SOLARA exists to make sure that changes going forward.
